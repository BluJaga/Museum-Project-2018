as presented by Kyle Bradburn




The big five for this unit are...


Image Topic Description
Louise Brooks Louise was a famous actress and dancer during the 1920s. She was a symbol of the flapper girl era and also one of the very first modern day celebrities. In addition, she popularized the bobbed haircut.
Aaron Douglas Aaron Douglas was a well known black artist during the 1920’s. Douglas was one of the most well known artists of the Harlem Renaissance. Douglas played a massive role in defining the culture of African Americans within the United States and moving the nation closer to a truly equal status.
Great Depression Following the Stock Market crash, the economic prosperity of the 1920’s came to an abrupt end. Banks began closing and more and more people lost jobs. Even more lost everything after the dust bowl ravaged the nation. Many people had no real home to live in. Herbert Hoover, the president at the time, did not work to fix the economy in time to fix the economy. Because of all these factors, the United States experienced a prolonged period of poverty.
Franklin Delano Roosevelt FDR was partially responsible for carrying the United States out of the Great Depression by implementing his New Deal. The New Deal emphasized three main points, relief, recovery, and reform. The New Deal established several federal agencies such as the FDIC, AAA, and social security. He also was responsible for calming the nation through his fireside chats.
Al Capone Al Capone had a huge economic impact on the United States during the Prohibition era of the United States. Despite the sale of alcohol being made illegal, Al Capone set up his gang in order to profit off of the lack of alcohol within the states. Through gang activity, Al Capone grew to be one of the wealthiest men within the nation during his time as a gangster. Despite the fact that he participated in illegal activities, Al Capone had a massive impact on the economy.
